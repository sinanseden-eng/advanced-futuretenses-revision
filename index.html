Future Tenses Revision

Future Continuous (will be doing)

The action is not complete when another action happens in the future. This is similar to past continuous, but it refers to the future.

Visual Timeline

NOW
Future Time
will be doing
"when you arrive"

Structure Summary

S + will + be + V-ing

Example: I will be singing at the concert tomorrow.

S + will + not + be + V-ing

Example: I will not be singing at the concert tomorrow.

Will + S + be + V-ing?

Example: Will you be singing at the concert tomorrow?

How & When to Use

1. To describe interrupted actions in the future

"When you come tomorrow, they will be playing tennis."

"At 8 PM tonight, they will be watching a movie when the power outage interrupts..."

"I will be studying when you call tonight."

2. To express actions in progress at a specific time

"At 12 o’clock tomorrow, we will be having lunch at school."

"At 9 PM tomorrow, I will be studying for my exam."

"This time next week, we will be flying to Paris."

3. To refer to actions continuing (with "still")

"Unfortunately, sea levels will still be rising in 20 years."

"He will be working on his thesis all night; the deadline is approaching."

"In ten years, people will still be using smartphones."

4. To ask a polite question about the future

"Will you be bringing your friend to the party tonight?"

"Will he be joining us for dinner tonight?"

"Will you be needing the car this evening?"

5. To emphasize future plans and intentions

"He'll be coming to visit us next week."

"Next weekend, they will be volunteering at the local shelter..."

"The mayor will be opening the new park next Tuesday."

6. To describe the atmosphere in the future

"When I arrive at the party, everybody will be celebrating..."

"Tomorrow night, the city will be buzzing with excitement..."

"At the party, John will be mixing drinks, and Maria will be greeting guests."

7. To express parallel actions in the future

"She will be watching TV, and he will be cooking dinner."

"Tomorrow morning, while I will be working..., my colleague will be preparing..."

"This evening, I will be reading while my partner will be cooking."

Time Expressions

In the future Next week Next time Next year At this moment, next year At this time tomorrow At 5 p.m tomorrow…

Adverb Placement

Adverbs can be placed in various positions to add meaning or emphasis.

1. Before the auxiliary verb “will”:

"Tomorrow, I will eagerly be attending the conference."

2. Between “will” and “be”:

"Sarah will be diligently studying for her exams all night." (Note: The user text example was for a different tense, this example is corrected for Future Continuous)

3. After the main verb:

"They will be working tirelessly on the project." (Note: The user's example "will have been" was Future Perfect Continuous, this is the Future Continuous equivalent)

4. At the end of the sentence:

"In a year’s time, we’ll still be living in this neighborhood happily."

5. At the beginning of the sentence:

"Gradually, the team will be adapting to the new management style."

Future Perfect (will have done)

The action will be completed in the future before another action or a specific time.

Visual Timeline

NOW
By 8 o'clock
X will have finished

Structure Summary

S + will + have + past participle

Example: I will have finished my report by the end of this month.

S + will + not + have + past participle

Example: He will not have done it by this evening.

Will + S + have + past participle?

Example: Will you have written me a letter?

How & When to Use

1. To talk about an action that will finish before a certain time in the future

"By eight o’clock tomorrow, I'll have taken off for Japan."

"By the end of the year, I will have saved $5,000."

"By midnight, all the guests will have left."

2. To talk about an action that will be completed before another event takes place

"She will have learned Chinese before she moves to China."

"They will have finished dinner by the time we get there."

"The movie will have started before we arrive at the cinema."

3. To express conviction that something happened in the near past

"The guests will have arrived at the hotel by now. (I’m sure they have arrived.)"

"Don't call her now; she will have gone to bed by this hour."

Time Expressions

By + … By the time Before Until/till By then By the end of + … By tomorrow

Future Perfect Continuous (will have been doing)

Action will continue in the future when it is interrupted by another action. This tense often includes an indication of how long the action has been happening.

Visual Timeline

NOW
By next month
will have been living (for 10 years)

Structure Summary

S + will + have + been + V-ing

Example: We will have been living in this house for 10 years by next month.

S + will + not + have + been + V-ing

Example: I won’t have been traveling to New York for 2 years by the end of October.

Will + S + have + been + V-ing?

Example: Will you have been eating vegetables for three months?

How & When to Use

1. To show something will continue up until a particular event or time in the future

"James will have been teaching at the university for more than a year by the time he leaves for Asia."

"By the time he retires, he will have been working here for 30 years."

"Next month, we will have been living together for a decade."

2. To talk about something that finishes just before another time or action (to emphasize duration)

"I will be tired when I get home because I will have been walking for over an hour."

"Her eyes will be red because she will have been crying."

"When I see you, I'll be exhausted as I will have been traveling for 24 hours."

Time Expressions

By + this year/month/week Next year/month/week For + an hour, for 5 years By then Since morning, since 1995 By tomorrow

Tense Comparison

Use these tables to quickly compare the structure and main uses of each future tense.

At a Glance

Tense Structure Main Use Example
Future Continuous S + will + be + V-ing An ongoing action at a specific point in the future. At 10 PM tonight, I will be watching a movie.
Future Perfect S + will + have + Past Participle An action completed *before* a specific point in the future. By 10 PM tonight, I will have watched the movie.
Future Perfect Continuous S + will + have + been + V-ing The duration of an action up to a specific point in the future. By 10 PM tonight, I will have been watching movies for 3 hours.

See how each tense changes the meaning of a sentence for a specific future point.

Tense Focus Example Sentence Meaning
Future Continuous Action in Progress "...I will be studying." At 9 PM, I will be in the middle of studying.
Future Perfect Action is Finished "...I will have studied." My studying will be completed *before* 9 PM.
